Constrained Policy Optimization (CPO) and Safe RL

Constrained Policy Optimization (CPO) and Safe RL

May 20, 20264 min read

  • robotics/control
  • math/optimization
  • blog-candidate

Summary

Reward-shaping penalties give no per-iteration safety guarantee and introduce an untunable scale hyperparameter. CMDPs formalize safety as hard budget constraints Jci​​(π)≤di​, and CPO solves each policy update as a trust-region QP that enforces those constraints while maintaining monotone reward improvement. The result is a policy update with O(δ​)-bounded expected constraint violation per iteration and a recovery rule for infeasible iterates.

Core Mechanism

CMDP formulation

πmax​Jr​(π)s.t.Jci​​(π):=Eτ∼π​[t∑​γtci​(st​,at​)]≤di​,∀i

Surrogate + KL bound (the trust-region motor)

Performance-difference identity (Kakade–Langford):

J(π′)−J(π)=1−γ1​Es∼dπ′,a∼π′​[Aπ(s,a)]

Replace dπ′ with dπ (importance-sampled) to get the computable surrogate:

Lπ​(π′)=1−γ1​Es∼dπ,a∼π​[π(a∣s)π′(a∣s)​Aπ(s,a)]

with bound J(π′)≥Lπ​(π′)−CEs∼dπ​[KL(π′∥π)(s)].

CPO update step (linearise objective and constraints, quadratic KL):

θk+1​=argθmax​g⊤(θ−θk​) s.t.21​(θ−θk​)⊤F(θ−θk​)≤δ bi⊤​(θ−θk​)+ei​≤0,ei​:=Jci​​(θk​)−di​

where g=∇θ​Jr​, bi​=∇θ​Jci​​, F = Fisher information matrix.

Δθ=ν1​F−1(g−∑i​λi​bi​)

ν,{λi​} from a small dual QP (analytic for m=1). F−1v computed via conjugate gradient — no matrix inversion.

Infeasibility recovery (when Jci​​(πk​)>di​):

Δθrec=−bi⊤​F−1bi​2δ​​F−1bi​

i.e., the steepest KL-step that reduces the constraint. Theorem: violation bounded O(δ​) per iteration.

∇θ​L=∇θ​Jr​−i∑​λi​∇θ​Jci​​,λi​←[λi​+η(Jci​​−di​)]+​

Stooke 2020: replace scalar η with a PID controller on λ to suppress oscillation.

Discrete-time analogue: enforce h(xk+1​)≥(1−η)h(xk​); becomes a QCQP when h is concave quadratic and dynamics are affine.

Why It Matters

On a humanoid (Unitree G1 class): torque limits, friction-cone margins, and fall avoidance are non-negotiable hard constraints, not “soft preferences.” Reward shaping with a penalty r−βc has no per-rollout guarantee — β is scale-dependent, tail violations are unbounded, and a single bad update can collapse the gait. CPO lets you train with Jc​≤d enforced in expectation at every gradient step, so the constraint budget is a tunable engineering parameter (e.g., d=0.05 torque-saturation rate) rather than an implicit consequence of β. The CBF layer can then be stacked on top to handle per-step set-invariance (friction cone) without retraining.

Limitations

  • Linearisation error: g, bi​ are first-order Taylor truncations of the true objective/constraint surfaces. The O(δ​) bound degrades when curvature is high (dense contact transitions, discontinuous reward).
  • Expectation-level safety only: Jci​​(π)≤di​ is an average. Rare catastrophic events (spike torques, stumble recovery) are not bounded in probability — CVaR or chance constraints would be needed for tail safety.
  • Fisher approximation cost: CG solve per update is O(nθ​⋅kCG​); expensive for large networks. PPO-Lagrangian is faster but weaker.
  • Stochastic policy requirement: CMDP optimal policy may be stochastic even when the unconstrained optimum is deterministic. Deterministic actor architectures (DDPG-style) are incompatible without modification.
  • No inter-sample guarantee for discrete CBF: the linearised discrete CBF is tight only at the prediction step; at 100 Hz actuation, inter-sample violation is non-negligible (moot at 1 kHz).

Open Questions

  • Residual-RL constraint placement: for a policy π=πMPC​+δπ, constraining δπ alone gives tighter formal bounds (MPC feasibility domain is known) but requires the MPC to be admissible at all times. Constraining total action is easier to implement but widens the feasible set.
  • CPO + CBF composition: CPO provides monotone improvement in expectation; CBF provides per-step set-invariance. The composition — run CPO offline, deploy with CBF filter online — is sound but the CBF distorts the action distribution the policy was trained under, creating a train/deploy mismatch. How large is that gap?
  • CVaR constraints in the CMDP: replacing Jci​​≤d with a CVaR constraint changes the dual variable structure; the Lagrangian is no longer linear in the policy and the conjugate-gradient trick breaks. What’s the right surrogate?
  • Early termination vs. finite λ: ET = λ→∞ kills gradient through the constraint manifold; Safety-Gym benchmarks show 3–5× slower learning. Is there a curriculum schedule λ(k) that recovers ET’s conservatism late in training while preserving CPO’s gradient flow early?
