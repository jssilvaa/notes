Observability of the Pseudo-Measurement and EKF Convergence

Summary

The contact wrench pseudo-measurement W^=(J(q)⊤)+(M(q)q¨​+h(q,q˙​)−S⊤τcmd​) loses rank at kinematic singularities, making certain force directions unobservable to the EKF. Standard observability theory (linear Gramian, nonlinear Lie-derivative rank condition) provides the diagnostic; the fix is a damped pseudo-inverse plus Q-projection in the Riccati update to stop noise from inflating along unobservable directions. Persistence of excitation is the system-identification analogue: wrench identification requires diverse contact accelerations spanning the full generalised velocity space.

Core Mechanism

Linear observability. System x˙=Ax+Bu, y=Cx. The observability matrix:

O=​CCACA2⋮CAn−1​​∈Rnm×n

(A,C) is observable iff rank(O)=n. Equivalent PBH test: rank[λI−AC​]=n for every eigenvalue λ. Unobservable subspace = kerO.

For LTV systems, replace with the observability Gramian:

WO​(t0​,t1​)=∫t0​t1​​Φ(t)⊤C⊤CΦ(t)dt

Uniform complete observability ⟺ WO​ uniformly positive definite.

Nonlinear observability (Hermann–Krener 1977). System x˙=f(x,u), y=h(x). Lie derivatives:

Lf​h(x)=∇h(x)⊤f(x,u),Lfk​h recursively

Observation space O(x)=span{dh,dLf​h,dLf2​h,…}. Local weak observability at x0​: dimO(x0​)=n.

Empirical observability Gramian (Krener–Ide 2009) for numerical evaluation:

Wijemp​=∫0T​(2ϵ)2(yi+​(t)−yi−​(t))(yj+​(t)−yj−​(t))​dt

where y± are trajectories from x0​±ϵei,j​. Converges to the analytical Gramian as ϵ→0.

Pseudo-measurement rank deficiency. The contact wrench estimate:

W^=(J(q)⊤)+(M(q)q¨​+h(q,q˙​)−S⊤τcmd​)

J(q)∈R6nc​×nv​. When J is rank-deficient, null directions of J⊤ correspond to internal forces (e.g. leg pushing against pelvis) invisible to q¨​. Key singularities on G1:

  • Knee fully extended → stance Jacobian rank drops by 1.
  • Both feet flat and parallel → yaw moment collapses.
  • Single-support with hip axis yaw-aligned with ankle axis.

Fix: damped pseudo-inverse with condition-number-adapted regularisation:

(JJ⊤+αI)−1J,α=α(cond(J))

EKF convergence under partial observability. Riccati update:

Pk+1​=(I−Kk+1​Ck+1​)Fk​Pk​Fk⊤​+Q

If (F,C) is uniformly completely observable, Pk​→P∞​ (bounded). Loss of observability in some directions ⇒ Pk​ unbounded along the unobservable subspace unless Q has no support there. Fix (Krener 2003): project Q onto the observable subspace — inject process noise only where the measurement can see it:

Qproj​=Πobs​QΠobs⊤​,Πobs​=row(Ck​) projector

Persistence of excitation (PE). For the adaptive identifier θ^˙=−γϕ(t)e(t), convergence θ^→θ⋆ requires:

∃α>0,T>0 s.t. ∫tt+T​ϕϕ⊤ds⪰αI∀t

A single push recovery event fails PE: the regressor ϕ has rank 1 for a ∼0.1 s window. Diverse contact accelerations spanning Rnv​ are required. Diagnostic: monitor the smallest eigenvalue of T1​∫0T​ϕϕ⊤dt — below ∼10−3 signals rank deficiency.

CoM observability from encoders only. Output y=qjoint​; state includes qbase​∈SE(3). CoM c=∑i​mi​ri​(q)/M depends on qbase​ which is invisible to encoders — global SE(3) ambiguity. With IMU: qbaserot​ observable up to yaw drift. With contact constraint (one foot flat): base position pinned; CoM fully observable. Double-support transition is the most observable moment; flight phase is the worst.

Why It Matters

In a state estimator for a legged robot, the EKF is fed a wrench pseudo-measurement derived from the contact Jacobian. At the exact instant the robot straightens its knee to transition between crouching and standing, J loses rank and the measurement noise in the yaw-moment channel becomes infinite — but the Riccati equation doesn’t know that unless you detect it. Without Q-projection, the EKF injects noise into the yaw moment state, the downstream MPC sees spurious disturbances, and the robot makes unnecessary corrective steps. Detecting the rank drop and projecting Q eliminates this without changing the estimator architecture.

Limitations

  • The empirical observability Gramian requires running perturbed trajectories, which is expensive on hardware and infeasible in real-time — it is a design-time diagnostic, not an online tool.
  • Damped pseudo-inverse with fixed α trades rank-deficiency robustness for bias in the wrench estimate; adapting α to the condition number requires online singular value computation.
  • Q-projection requires detecting rank-deficient Ck​ at every EKF step; numerical rank determination has tolerance sensitivity near singularities.
  • PE for wrench identification requires deliberate excitation (chirp + perturbations); closed-loop walking alone is insufficient and combining datasets introduces domain mismatch.
  • Linear EKF observability analysis around x^k​ is only locally valid; the nonlinear Lie-derivative rank condition is the correct global check but harder to compute online.

Open Questions

  • Can the controller deliberately excite observability by injecting a small probing signal during quiet phases (dual control / Bar-Shalom)? The cost is non-zero perturbation of the nominal trajectory; quantify the trade-off as a constrained optimisation over probing amplitude.
  • If W^ is unobservable in one direction, a learned actuator network trained on W^ will see zero gradient in that direction during training — it will not generalise to perturbations along the unobservable subspace. This is a principled failure mode, not a generalisation gap.
  • Is the empirical observability Gramian related to the neural tangent kernel of a network trained on the same trajectories? Both measure how output variation relates to parameter/state perturbation — the connection may yield a learning-based observability diagnostic.
  • What is the simplest 1-D worked example of dual control (Bar-Shalom)? A scalar system with unknown gain and a cost that penalises both tracking error and estimation variance would make the exploration-exploitation trade-off explicit.
