OptNet: Differentiable Optimization as a Layer in Neural Networks

OptNet: Differentiable Optimization as a Layer in Neural Networks

May 26, 20264 min read

  • math/optimization
  • robotics/control

Summary

Standard neural network layers cannot enforce hard constraints between hidden states. OptNet embeds a quadratic program (QP) as a differentiable layer, making the layer output the solution z∗ of a constrained optimization problem parameterized by the preceding layer. Gradients flow through the layer via implicit differentiation of the KKT conditions, and a custom GPU-batched primal-dual interior point solver makes the backward pass essentially free once the forward solve is done.

Core Mechanism

The OptNet layer solves:

zi+1​=argzmin​21​zTQ(zi​)z+q(zi​)Tzs.t.A(zi​)z=b(zi​),G(zi​)z≤h(zi​)

Backward pass via KKT differentiation. At the optimal (z∗,ν∗,λ∗), differentiate the stationarity, primal feasibility, and complementary slackness conditions to get:

​QD(λ∗)GA​GTD(Gz∗−h)0​AT00​​​dzdλdν​​=−RHS(dQ,dq,dG,dh,dA,db)

For backprop, solve the transpose of this system once against ∂ℓ/∂z∗, then read off all parameter gradients:

∇Q​ℓ=21​(dzz∗T+z∗dzT),∇q​ℓ=dz,∇b​ℓ=−dν,∇h​ℓ=−D(λ∗)dλ,…

Solver. A GPU-batched primal-dual interior point method (PDIPM) following Mattingley & Boyd. Each iteration solves:

K​ΔzaffΔsaffΔλaffΔνaff​​=−rKKT​

The LU factorization of Ksym​ at convergence is reused for the backward pass linear solve — no second factorization required. Batch size 128, 100-variable QPs: 0.18s vs Gurobi’s 4.7s.

Representational power. OptNet ⊇ ReLU (can encode max{Wz+b,0} as a QP with O(mn) params). Conversely, max{a1T​x,a2T​x,a3T​x} is a single OptNet layer but requires O(cp) ReLU units to approximate — ReLU creases span all of Rn; QP feasibility region creases can be bounded.

Why It Matters

This lets you embed known hard constraints (physical feasibility, logical rules, safety bounds) directly into the computation graph where previously you had to either hand-engineer a post-processing projection (not differentiable end-to-end) or hope the network learned to satisfy them implicitly (it won’t). Concrete: in the Sudoku experiment, a 10-layer CNN with 512 filters per layer overfits and fails to generalize to unseen puzzles; a single OptNet layer with a learnable Ax=b recovers near-perfect generalization — because row/column/box exclusivity are hard linear constraints, not soft patterns.

Signal denoising: initialize the OptNet difference operator D to the TV solution, fine-tune end-to-end → 12% test MSE improvement. You couldn’t do this with a fixed convex program.

Limitations

  • Cubic scaling: QP solve is O(n3) in variables/constraints. Practically limited to n<1000; below 100 for real-time. This rules out any application with high-dimensional decision spaces.
  • Dense matrices only: The solver exploits GPU-batched dense BLAS. Real-world problems with sparse constraint structure (e.g., MPC with sparse dynamics) don’t benefit and are penalized by dense factorization overhead.
  • Training instability: The QP parameter manifold has gauge redundancies (scaling constraint rows doesn’t change the solution set), so gradient landscapes are degenerate. More tuning required than FC/conv layers.
  • Complementary slackness degeneracy: At points where λi∗​=0 and (Gz∗−h)i​=0 simultaneously, the KKT matrix is singular — the layer is subdifferentiable but not differentiable, and gradient-based training becomes unreliable.

Open Questions

  • Can structured (sparse, banded) QP solvers be integrated while maintaining GPU batch efficiency? The authors mention cuSOLVER sparse QR as a start.
  • How does this interact with stochastic training when QP feasibility depends on learned parameters — are there initialization strategies that guarantee feasibility throughout training?
  • For MPC-in-the-loop robotics: can the O(n3) barrier be broken by exploiting time-structured (block-banded) KKT systems from the MPC horizon?
  • Learnable Q: parameterizing via Cholesky Q=LLT+ϵI ensures PSD but doubles the parameter count. Are there better parameterizations for structured physics-inspired Q?
