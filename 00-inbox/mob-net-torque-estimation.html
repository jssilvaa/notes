MOB Net: Limb modularized Uncertainty Torque Learning of Humanoids for Sensorless External Torque Estimation

MOB-Net: Limb-modularized Uncertainty Torque Learning of Humanoids for Sensorless External Torque Estimation

May 26, 20266 min read

  • robotics/control
  • robotics/perception
  • embedded/hardware

Summary

The momentum observer (MOB) estimates external joint torques without force/torque sensors (FTS), but its residual signal is corrupted by model uncertainty — lumped modeling error, nonlinear joint friction, and sensor noise — which makes it unusable for collision detection on walking humanoids. MOB-Net wraps the MOB residual inside a limb-modularized GRU architecture that learns to predict and subtract the uncertainty torque, leaving a clean external torque estimate with RMSE reduced from 19.30 Nm (MOB alone) to 1.31 Nm (near the sensor-noise floor) in simulation. The estimated torque and its predicted variance are then used for collision detection thresholding and three full collision-reaction scenarios on a walking full-size humanoid (TOCABI, 1.8m, 100kg).

Core Mechanism

MOB baseline. Floating-base dynamics in generalized coordinates (n+6 DoF including 6 virtual joints at the pelvis):

M(qv​)q¨​v​+C(qv​,q˙​v​)q˙​v​+g(qv​)=τv​+τe​

Generalized momentum p=M(qv​)q˙​v​; its derivative is:

p˙​=τv​+τe​+β(qv​,q˙​v​),β=C⊤q˙​v​−g(qv​)

The MOB residual vector (avoids noisy q¨​ by integrating):

r=K0​(p(t)−p(0)−∫0t​(τv​+β+r)dt)

Per-joint transfer function: ri​(s)/τe,i​(s)=k0,i​/(s+k0,i​) — MOB is a first-order low-pass of the true external torque. In practice: r=τ~e​+τ~u​, where ⋅~ denotes low-pass filtered.

Uncertainty torque decomposition. Three additive sources:

τu​(qv​,q˙​v​,q¨​v​,τv​)=model errorτp​​​+friction (incl. load-dep.)τf​(qv​,q˙​v​,τv​)​​+sensor noiseτn​​​

Leg friction is load-dependent (Stribeck + klf​∣τm​∣2 term) due to 100:1 harmonic gear under >1000N load; arm friction depends only on velocity. This asymmetry drives the modular architecture.

Problem formulation. Estimate the per-joint Gaussian distribution of uncertainty torque from proprioception Xp​:

τ^u∗​(Xp​),σ^u2∗​(Xp​)=argτ^u​,σ^u2​max​P(τu​∣τ^u​,σ^u​,Xp​)

Solved by minimizing Gaussian negative log-likelihood:

L=i,j∑​[lnσ^u,j,θ2​+σ^u,j,θ2​(τ^u,j,θ​−τu,ji​)2​]

Output: τ~e​=r−τ^u​ (pure external torque). Variance σ^u​ is used as an adaptive collision detection threshold.

Limb modularization. The inertia matrix M(qv​) is sparse: off-diagonal blocks are zero between non-adjacent limb chains. Six independent GRU-Linear networks, one per limb group g∈{V,RL,LL,W,RA,LA}, each receiving only kinematically relevant joints. Input per step:

xg​(k)=[Qg​(k),Tg​(k),UV(k)]

where Qg​=[qg​,q˙​g​], Tg​=[τdg​] (desired torque, legs only), UV=[pRfb,1:6​,ωfb​,v˙fb​] (pelvis IMU). Joint acceleration is dropped (too noisy); its information is recovered implicitly by the GRU’s hidden state over a 50-100 step TBPTT horizon. Total network parameters: ~777K across all six networks; inference <1ms on a single Intel i7 core.

Training data. Legs: random walking data on uneven terrain (wooden plates 10mm, 3° slope) + Random Torque Exploration (RTE) — step-function random torques up to 50Nm added to joints to cover the velocity/torque distributions encountered during collisions. Target: r−τem​ (residual minus FTS-measured external torque). Upper body: free teleoperated motion, target: r directly (no contact). ~3.6M samples per set, 1kHz.

Collision detection. Threshold on ∣τ~e​∣ is replaced with a probability-based condition using the estimated variance: collision is flagged when ∣τ~e​∣>η⋅σ^u​ for tunable η, making the sensitivity adaptive to the current uncertainty level rather than a fixed global threshold.

Why It Matters

This lets a humanoid detect and react to unexpected collisions during walking without FTS anywhere except at the feet for data collection — the feet FTS are needed for training supervision but not at deployment. Previously the two options were: (a) FTS on every link (expensive, adds inertia, fails at occluded contacts), or (b) model-based MOB alone (RMSE 19 Nm — unusable as a collision signal because ground reaction forces during heel-strike produce similar magnitudes). The variance output doubles as a detection threshold, so you don’t need to manually retune a fixed threshold for each terrain or gait mode — the network tells you how uncertain it is.

Concrete scenario enabled: a walking humanoid’s foot collides with a heavy box in a camera blind spot. The external torque estimate spikes at the foot/ankle virtual joints, the collision is detected within one control cycle, and a back-step reaction is executed. None of this was demonstrated on a full-size walking humanoid before this work.

Limitations

  • FTS still required for data collection on legs: the training target for leg joints is r−τem​ where τem​ comes from FTS. Platforms without foot FTS must use the hanging-robot alternative, which requires specialized infrastructure and ignores load-dependent friction.
  • Distribution shift at collision time: training data is collision-free (except ground reactions and RTE perturbations). RTE improves robustness but cannot cover all collision geometries; accuracy degrades for collisions that push joints outside the trained velocity/torque distribution — especially high-speed impacts.
  • No contact point estimation: the output is external torque in generalized coordinates. Mapping back to contact wrench in operational space requires solving an underdetermined system when contact location is unknown, which the paper does not address.
  • Fixed walking controller during data collection: all training data uses one specific walking controller. If a different gait or agile motion (jumping, running) is deployed, the uncertainty torque distribution changes and the network must be retrained.
  • Harmonic gear assumption: the load-dependent friction model targets high gear-ratio (100:1) actuators. For QDD or SEA actuators with near-zero friction, the leg input feature design (Tg​ included) may be unnecessary and the modular split may need revisiting.

Open Questions

  • Can the FTS supervision signal for leg training be replaced with contact force estimates from a separate foot contact estimator, making the whole pipeline truly sensor-free at both training and test time?
  • The RTE perturbations are heuristic (uniform random step functions up to 50Nm). Is there a principled way to design the exploration distribution — e.g., adversarially sampling torques that maximize estimation error on a held-out collision set?
  • How does MOB-Net compose with a whole-body controller that actively changes the desired torque τd​ (like the ViC-MPC contact wrench optimizer)? The input Tg​ feeds current desired torque, so rapid MPC-driven torque changes might confuse the GRU’s hidden state.
  • The limb modularity exploits the sparsity of M(qv​) — but C(qv​,q˙​v​) has Coriolis terms that couple limbs during fast motions. At what motion speed does this approximation break, and can cross-limb attention (sparse transformer) recover the missing coupling without abandoning the real-time budget?
